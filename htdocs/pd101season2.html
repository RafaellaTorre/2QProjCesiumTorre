PRODUCE 101

season 2

Description

Airing every friday from April 7 to June 16, Produce 101 Season 2 is the follow-up to Mnet's hit reality-survival show, Produce 101 season 1 and also serves as the male counterpart of the earlier season. Just like the first, Produce 101 season 2 also maintains the goal of debuting an 11-membered group out of the 101 trainees from 54 different entertainment companies. In a similar manner, the final members for the debut group consists of the top 11 trainees based on each individual's votes from the season's finale episode. This season encouraged more independent trainees (trainees without a company) to start auditioning on reality-survival shows; after seeing the success of Kim Jaehwan who ranked 4 and was able to debut in the program's project boy group, Wanna One. Additionally, Lee Daehwi, who auditioned with his self-composed song, Hollywood, also paved a path for trainees in the later seasons to do the same, specially as his song impressed the mentors very much.

Some notable trainees in this season are:

  • Lee Daehwi - who was the very first trainee to audition with a self-composed song and this season's center for "Pick Me"
  • Kim Jaehwan - who was the very first independent trainee to debut
  • Jang Moon Bok -
  • Kim Samuel - who was supposed to debut with the boy group "Seventeen" before participating in the show

Wanna One

Wanna One (stylized:워너원) was derived from the English pronounciation of 101 and also holds the meaning "wanna become one." The group debuted by releasing their mini-album 1X1=1 (also known as To Be One) on August 7, 2017 with the title track "Energetic" and also including the acoustic version of "Always", a song from Produce 101 Season 2's last episode. The group peaked after their release of the "Boomerang" and "I.P.U" under their album 0+1=1(I Promise You) on March 19, 2018, with the two songs charting for over a hundred days on Melon and the album charting as the highest sold album on Hanteo's realtime chart a day after its release. From then on the group continued with their success, their next album, 1÷x=1 (Undivided) achieved a "realtime all-kill" on Korean Music Charts a day after its release. Although the group was originally set to disband on December 30, 2017, their contract was extended and the group held their disbanment concert, "Therefore", on the end of January 2019. The last album they released was [] with the title track []

Following the group's disbandment, the members each returned to their respective agencies with Kang Daniel and Yoon Jisung moving to LM Entertainment from MMO Entertainment. A few months later, Kang Daniel sued LM Entertainment for selling his contract without consent and left the company to establish his own, whilst Yoon Jisung stayed in the company and debuted as a soloist on April 25, 2019 with Kang Daniel following suit on July 25,2019. Park Jihoon released the song "L.O.V.E", marking his debut as a soloist on March 26, 2019; he also recenty picked up acting again (he used to be a child actor) appearing in the drama "Flower Crew:Joseon Marriage Agency." Lee Daehwi and Park Woojin, along with three other labelmates debuted on May 22,2019 in a group named AB6IX. Kim Jaehwan, who was an independent trainee, signed with Swing Entertainment and made his solo debut on May 20, 2019. Ong Seongwoo focused on acting for a while, appearing on "Moment of Eighteen", and "More than Friends", on January 9, 2020 he finally made his solo debut with the digital single "We Belong." Lai Kuanlin left Cube Entertainment on July 2019 and is currently working as a singer and actor in Taiwan. Hwang Minhyun returned to his previous group, NUEST and they made a comeback with their mini-album "Happily Ever After" on April 29, 2019. Bae Jinyoung debuted under the group CIX, with 5 other labelmates on July 23, 2019. Lastly, Ha Sungwoon made his solo debut on February 28, 2019 and recently transferred to Big Planet Made (BPM) in 2021.
