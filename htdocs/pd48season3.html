PRODUCE 48

season 3

Description

Airing on Fridays of 2018, from June 15 to August 31, Produce 48 is the third season of Mnet's Produce franchise, unlike the other programs under the franchise, the numbers 101 cannot be found in the title and is instead replaced by the numbers 48, derived from the name of the Japanese idol group AKB48, who Mnet collaborated with, in the making of this season. In contrary to the title, there are actually 96 trainees who participated in this season, 48 coming from South Korean companies and the remaining 48 from the members of AKB48 and their sister girlgroups (e.g. HKT48,NMB48, etc.). The program still follows the usual flow of the 2 previous seasons, however, instead of debuting an 11-membered group, this season aims to debut a 12-membered girlgroup that would promote for 2 and a half years, in both South Korea and Japan, and is the only season out of all the four to do so. Not only this, but the program had also made adjustments by adding Japanese songs for the evaluations and also adding a Japanese version to "Nekkoya", this season's version of the first two season's "Pick Me".

Some notable trainees in this season are:

  • Miyawaki Sakura - who was a high-ranking member of HKT48 for 2 years in a row and was predicted to win the next Senbatsu election, she was the center for Nekkoya and made the belief amongst the show's fans that the center for each season's "Pick Me" will debut
  • Matsui Jurina - one of the most senior members of SKE48 and had placed first on all Senbatsu Elections since 2009
  • Jang Wonyoung - who got the nickname "Born-to-be Center" because of her amazing facial expression and the fact that she was a center during the group and concept evaluations
  • Jang Gyuri - who was currently in fromis_9, a girlgroup from another Mnet reality survival show, who had just debuted on January 8 of the same year
  • An Yujin - a trainee who had appeared on many commercials, including a cosmetics commercial with Astro's Cha Eunwoo and Ailee's music video for "Thirst" to promote Gatorade
  • Kim Minju - a trainee who had recently acted in the kdrama "Tempted"
  • Lee Chaeyeon - who got the nickname "survival show addict" as she had alreay appeared in two other survival shows, namely, "K-pop Star 3" and "Sixteen"

IZ*ONE

IZ*ONE (stylized: 아이즈원) is a combination of "IZ" which symbolizes 12 and "ONE" to show that the 12 members becoming one to from IZ*ONE. The group debuted in Korea on October 29, 2018 by releasing their mini-album "COLOR*IZ", featuring the group's version of the three songs performed on Produce 48's final episode and Nekkoya as a CD-only track, the mini-album broke the record for the highest number of albums sold both on the first day and the first week sales of a girlgroup debut album. On February 5, 2019, they digitally released their first Japanese single, Suki to Iwasetai, and on the following day, they officially debuted and the physical copies of the album were released. On the group's first anniversary, they released the concept trailer for their first full album, "BLOOM*IZ" which was supposed to be released on November 11, 2019, however due to the Produce Rigging Controversy, the release was postponed and the group went on a 3-month hiatus, coming back on midnight of February 3, 2020 (KST) by releasing the album's cover image. Although the group was set to disband on April 29, 2021, they held their 2-day disbandment concert on March 13 and 14, 2021, which also marked the last activity they had as group (everything released afterwards was prerecorded) and the group's farewell message was posted on their official accounts on the midnight of May 1, 2021 (KST). The last album they released was "One-Reeler Act IV" on December 7, 2020 and the last song they officially released was "3!4!" for the project Rewind : Blossom.

After the disbandment, Jang Wonyoung and An Yujin debuted with 4 other labelmates on December 1, 2021 under Starship Entertainment's girlgroup "IVE". Miyawaki Sakura announced her graduation from HKT48 and held her graduation concert, "Bouquet" on June 19, 2021. Jo Yuri was transferred from Stone Entertainment to WAKEONE Entertainment, another sublabel of CJ ENM; she recorded an OST for the drama "Monthly Magazine" and made her solo debut with the single album "Glassy" on October 7, 2021. Choi Yena continued on with her career as an entertainer, she became a regular of Girls High School Mystery Class 2 and started her own show titled "Animal Detective"; she also debuted as an actress by starring in the webdrama "The World of My 17 Season 2" and she debuted as a soloist on January 17, 2022 by releasing her mini-album ˣ‿ˣ (SMiLEY). Kwon Eunbi released her mini-album "DOOR" and debuted as a soloist on August 24, 2021. Kang Hyewon debuted as an actress in the 3rd season of the webdrama "Best Mistake" and released her song "Winter Poem" on December 22, 2021. Kim Minju continued with her job as an MC on MBC's Music Core, she also participated in a special stage in MBC Gayo Daejejeon where she covered f(x)'s "Rum Pum Pum Pum" with other girlgroup members, and she released a photobook containing pictures she took, titled "Pro Memoria". Lee Chaeyeon participated in yet another of one of Mnet's survival shows, Street Woman Fighter, whilst Honda Hitomi and Yabuki Nako both returned to AKB48 and HKT48, respectively. As for Kim Chaewon, there are no confirmed news as of now, however, she is rumored to have moved from Woollim Entertainment to HYBE Entertainment and is said to debut in their rumored girlgroup, "Lesserafim" alongside Miyawaki Sakura.
